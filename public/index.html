About this project!

This project is a demonstration of the exciting capabilities of WebGL to craft Virtual Reality experiences. Instead of shelling out thousands of dollars for an Oculus Rift or other mainline VR platform you can instead have similar experiences right on your phone! The growing capabilities of WebGL in the browser combined with the ever increasing performace of both desktop and mobile devices may one day revolutionize the way we experience the internet - from a 'marked up' document experience, to more graphics oriented interactions. This project leverages Mozilla's new MobileVR API to interface with the hardware on smartphones to craft a VR experience right from the mobile browser. By combining this with AFrame, a Web Graphics framework dedicated to Mobile VR, you can experience a tour of DePauw University right from your phone - all you need is an internet connection and a mobile device with a modern browser!

How to use this project!

Just tap/click on the 'Start' button or select a locaton from the dropdown to get started! To transition to the next location just keep your VR cursor focused on the location of interest (marked by a sphere) for 5 seconds. To return to the home page to select a location manually from the dropdown, simply stare at the 'X' rendered below you for 5 seconds as well. Otherwise, this experience behaves just like a regular website.

If you have Mobile VR goggles such as google cardboard, you can press the 'VR' button render a traditional VR view with your phone! If you want to go back after entering VR mode however, you will need to use your browser's back button.
